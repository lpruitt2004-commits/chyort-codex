# Art

Art is the expression of human creativity and imagination, encompassing a vast range of visual, auditory, and performance forms. It reflects culture, history, emotion, and innovation, shaping and being shaped by society.

## History & Evolution

- **Prehistoric Art:** Cave paintings (Lascaux, Altamira), petroglyphs, early sculpture (Venus figurines)
- **Ancient Art:** Egyptian murals, Greek sculpture, Roman mosaics, Chinese calligraphy, Mesoamerican temples
- **Medieval Art:** Illuminated manuscripts, Gothic cathedrals, Byzantine icons
- **Renaissance:** Perspective, anatomy, realism; da Vinci, Michelangelo, Raphael
- **Baroque & Rococo:** Dramatic lighting, ornate detail; Caravaggio, Rembrandt, Fragonard
- **19th Century:** Romanticism, Realism, Impressionism, Post-Impressionism; Monet, Van Gogh, Degas
- **Modernism:** Cubism, Surrealism, Abstract Expressionism; Picasso, Dali, Pollock
- **Contemporary:** Pop art, street art, digital art, installation; Warhol, Banksy, Kusama

## Notable Artists & Biographies

- **Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519):** Renaissance polymath, painter of the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, inventor, scientist
- **Michelangelo (1475–1564):** Sculptor of David, painter of the Sistine Chapel ceiling, architect
- **Claude Monet (1840–1926):** Founder of Impressionism, master of light and color
- **Pablo Picasso (1881–1973):** Pioneer of Cubism, prolific painter and sculptor
- **Frida Kahlo (1907–1954):** Mexican painter, known for self-portraits and exploration of identity
- **Yayoi Kusama (b. 1929):** Japanese contemporary artist, immersive installations, polka dots

## Timeline of Major Art Movements

- **30,000 BCE:** Oldest known cave paintings
- **3000 BCE:** Egyptian art and architecture
- **500 BCE:** Greek classical sculpture
- **500 CE:** Byzantine mosaics
- **1400–1600:** Renaissance
- **1600–1750:** Baroque
- **1800–1900:** Impressionism, Post-Impressionism
- **1900–1950:** Cubism, Surrealism, Abstract Expressionism
- **1950–present:** Pop art, Minimalism, Digital art

## Case Study: The Sistine Chapel Ceiling

Painted by Michelangelo between 1508 and 1512, the Sistine Chapel ceiling in Vatican City is a masterpiece of Renaissance art. It features scenes from Genesis, including the iconic Creation of Adam, and demonstrates mastery of anatomy, perspective, and narrative.

### Core Concepts

- **Drawing & Painting**: Techniques include charcoal, oil, watercolor, acrylics; composition principles like rule of thirds, perspective, balance; mediums affect texture and permanence
- **Sculpture**: Modeling with clay, carving stone/wood, casting in bronze/resin, installation art that transforms spaces
- **Art History**: Renaissance (rebirth of classical learning), Impressionism (light and color), Modernism (abstraction), Contemporary (digital/mixed media)
- **Digital Art**: Graphic design for visual communication, 3D modeling for games/film, animation for storytelling, vector vs. raster techniques
- **Photography**: Composition through framing, lighting manipulation for mood, aperture/shutter/ISO controls, digital post-processing with RAW editing
- **Color Theory**: Color wheels and harmonies (complementary, analogous, triadic), warm vs. cool tones, psychological effects of colors

### Great Artists & Movements

- **Renaissance Masters**: Leonardo da Vinci (anatomical precision), Michelangelo (sculpture/painting), Raphael (composition)
- **Impressionism**: Monet's light studies, Renoir's soft edges, Degas's movement capture
- **Modernism**: Picasso (cubism), Kandinsky (abstraction), Matisse (color experimentation)
- **Contemporary**: Banksy (street art/social commentary), Yayoi Kusama (immersive installations), Ai Weiwei (political activism)

### Skills & Resources

- **Essential Skills**: Observation, hand-eye coordination, creative problem-solving, understanding composition and proportion
- **Learning Paths**: Art schools (BFA programs), online platforms (Skillshare, Pluralsight), museums (study real works), practice communities
- **Tools**: Traditional (charcoal, oils, brushes) to digital (Photoshop, Procreate, Blender, ZBrush)

### Fields of Work

- **Fine Artist**: Create original paintings, sculptures, installations for galleries and collectors; build portfolio and market your work
- **Graphic Designer**: Design logos, branding, marketing materials; strong understanding of design software essential
- **Illustrator**: Create images for books, magazines, advertising; often freelance or contract-based
- **Art Director**: Oversee visual style for projects, campaigns, productions; requires both creative and management skills
- **Museum Curator**: Manage art collections, exhibitions, education programs; usually requires master's degree
- **Art Teacher**: Educate students in visual arts at K-12 or university level; share techniques and inspire creativity
- **Animator**: Create motion graphics, films, video games; requires both artistic and technical skills
- **Photographer**: Capture images for media, events, portraits; can specialize (wildlife, fashion, documentary)
