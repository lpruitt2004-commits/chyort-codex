# Engineering

Engineering is the creative application of science and mathematics to solve problems, design systems, and build the infrastructure of society. It spans ancient innovations to modern technology, shaping the world we live in.

## History & Evolution

- **Ancient Engineering:** Pyramids of Egypt, Roman aqueducts, Greek temples, Chinese Great Wall
- **Medieval:** Cathedrals, castles, watermills, Islamic architecture
- **Industrial Revolution:** Steam engines, railways, bridges, mass production
- **20th Century:** Skyscrapers, airplanes, electronics, computers, space exploration
- **21st Century:** Renewable energy, nanotechnology, biomedical devices, smart infrastructure

## Major Branches

- **Civil Engineering:** Structures, bridges, roads, dams, water systems
- **Mechanical Engineering:** Machines, engines, robotics, manufacturing
- **Electrical Engineering:** Circuits, power systems, electronics, telecommunications
- **Chemical Engineering:** Chemical processes, pharmaceuticals, materials
- **Computer Engineering:** Hardware, embedded systems, digital design
- **Aerospace Engineering:** Aircraft, spacecraft, satellites
- **Biomedical Engineering:** Medical devices, prosthetics, imaging, tissue engineering
- **Environmental Engineering:** Pollution control, waste management, sustainable design

## Notable Engineers & Biographies

- **Isambard Kingdom Brunel (1806–1859):** British civil engineer, bridges, tunnels, railways
- **Nikola Tesla (1856–1943):** Electrical engineering, AC power, radio
- **Hedy Lamarr (1914–2000):** Inventor, frequency-hopping technology (Wi-Fi precursor)
- **Elon Musk (b. 1971):** SpaceX, Tesla, modern engineering entrepreneurship
- **Mae Jemison (b. 1956):** Engineer, physician, first African American woman in space

## Timeline of Major Developments

- **~2600 BCE:** Egyptian pyramids
- **312 BCE:** Roman aqueducts
- **1769:** Watt’s steam engine
- **1888:** Tesla’s AC motor
- **1969:** Apollo 11 moon landing
- **2000s:** Rise of renewable energy, smart technology

## Case Study: The Brooklyn Bridge

Completed in 1883, the Brooklyn Bridge was a marvel of civil engineering, using steel cables and caissons. It connected Manhattan and Brooklyn, symbolizing innovation and urban growth.
