# Environmental Science

Environmental science is the interdisciplinary study of the environment, ecosystems, and human impacts on the planet. It integrates biology, chemistry, geology, and social sciences to address environmental challenges and promote sustainability.

## History & Evolution

- **Ancient & Indigenous Knowledge:** Early resource management, traditional ecological knowledge
- **19th Century:** Conservation movement, national parks, Thoreau’s "Walden"
- **20th Century:** Rachel Carson’s "Silent Spring," environmental laws, Earth Day, global awareness
- **21st Century:** Climate change science, renewable energy, sustainability, environmental justice

## Major Subfields

- **Ecology:** Study of organisms and their environments
- **Atmospheric Science:** Weather, climate, air pollution
- **Geoscience:** Soil, water, earth systems
- **Environmental Chemistry:** Pollutants, cycles, remediation
- **Conservation Biology:** Protecting species and habitats
- **Environmental Policy:** Laws, regulations, management

## Notable Environmental Scientists & Biographies

- **Rachel Carson (1907–1964):** Author of "Silent Spring," sparked modern environmental movement
- **Aldo Leopold (1887–1948):** Ecologist, land ethic, "A Sand County Almanac"
- **James Lovelock (1919–2022):** Gaia hypothesis, earth systems science
- **Wangari Maathai (1940–2011):** Environmentalist, Nobel Peace Prize, Green Belt Movement

## Timeline of Major Developments

- **~10,000 BCE:** Early agriculture and land management
- **1872:** Yellowstone, first national park
- **1962:** "Silent Spring" published
- **1970:** First Earth Day
- **1997:** Kyoto Protocol on climate change
- **2015:** Paris Agreement

## Case Study: The Montreal Protocol

Signed in 1987, the Montreal Protocol phased out ozone-depleting substances. It is considered one of the most successful international environmental agreements, leading to recovery of the ozone layer.
