# Political Science

Political science examines government, politics, and public policy to understand how societies organize power and make collective decisions.

## History

- **Ancient Roots:** Political thought in Greece (Plato, Aristotle), Rome (Cicero), India (Kautilya), and China (Confucius).
- **Enlightenment:** Hobbes, Locke, Rousseau, Montesquieu develop social contract theory, separation of powers, and rights.
- **Modern Era:** Rise of nation-states, democracy, political parties, and international organizations.
- **Contemporary:** Globalization, digital politics, new social movements, and transnational governance.

## Notable Figures

- **Plato:** Ideal state, philosopher-kings.
- **Aristotle:** Comparative government, natural state.
- **John Locke:** Natural rights, limited government.
- **Karl Marx:** Class struggle, communism.
- **Max Weber:** Bureaucracy, authority types.
- **Hannah Arendt:** Totalitarianism, political action.

## Timeline

- **c. 400 BCE:** Plato’s "Republic."
- **c. 350 BCE:** Aristotle’s "Politics."
- **1689:** Locke’s "Two Treatises of Government."
- **1848:** Marx and Engels publish "The Communist Manifesto."
- **1945:** United Nations founded.
- **1991:** End of the Cold War.

## Case Study: The Fall of the Berlin Wall

In 1989, the Berlin Wall fell, symbolizing the end of the Cold War and the triumph of democratic movements over authoritarian regimes in Eastern Europe. This event reshaped global politics and international relations.

## Knowledge Base

### Political Theory & Philosophy

- **Democracy:** Rule by the people; direct, representative, participatory
- **Authoritarianism:** Power concentrated in few; limited freedoms
- **Justice & Rights:** Natural rights, social contracts, equality
- **Power:** Distribution, legitimacy, consent
- **Sovereignty:** Ultimate authority; nation-states, supranational bodies
- **Freedom & Liberty:** Individual vs. collective needs

### Political Systems & Institutions

- **Democracies:** Checks and balances, elections, rule of law
- **Parliamentary Systems:** Prime minister, coalition governments
- **Presidential Systems:** President as head of state/government
- **Constitutional Monarchies:** Monarch as ceremonial head
- **Federal vs. Unitary:** Power sharing vs. centralization
- **Comparative Politics:** Cross-country analysis

### International Relations

- **State System:** Sovereign nation-states, anarchy
- **Diplomacy:** Negotiation, treaties, alliances
- **War & Conflict:** Causes, types, resolution
- **Global Organizations:** UN, NATO, EU, WTO
- **International Law:** Treaties, conventions
- **Geopolitics:** Geography, resources, power
- **Transnational Issues:** Climate, migration, terrorism

### Public Policy & Governance

- **Policy Making:** Problem identification, formulation, implementation
- **Law-Making:** Legislative process
- **Regulation:** Business, environment, society
- **Fiscal Policy:** Taxation, spending
- **Environmental Policy:** Climate action, conservation
- **Social Policy:** Healthcare, education, welfare

### Political Ideologies & Movements

- **Liberalism:** Individual rights, democracy
- **Conservatism:** Tradition, gradual change
- **Socialism/Communism:** Collective ownership, equality
- **Fascism:** Authoritarian nationalism
- **Libertarianism:** Maximum individual freedom
- **Populism:** "People" vs. "elites"
- **Environmentalism:** Sustainability, conservation
- **Feminism:** Gender equality

### Political Behavior & Voting

- **Voting Behavior:** Issues, candidates, party ID
- **Political Socialization:** Family, schools, media
- **Voting Systems:** First-past-the-post, proportional, ranked choice
- **Elections:** Campaigns, fundraising, media
- **Political Parties:** Organization, primaries, platforms
- **Interest Groups:** Advocacy, lobbying

### Skills & Resources

- **Essential Skills:** Critical thinking, analysis, writing, research, multiple perspectives
- **Learning Paths**: Political science degrees (BA/MA/PhD), law school, policy schools, journalism, internships in government
- **Tools**: Databases (JSTOR, ProQuest), statistical software, news literacy, primary source analysis
- **Career Prep**: Understand current issues deeply; public speaking; internships and networking; writing portfolio

### Fields of Work

- **Political Scientist**: Research political systems, behavior, institutions; teach and publish; universities, think tanks
- **Policy Analyst**: Evaluate and recommend public policies; research impacts; government agencies, think tanks, NGOs
- **Diplomat**: Represent country in international relations; negotiate treaties; foreign service
- **Campaign Manager**: Organize political campaigns; strategy, messaging, volunteer coordination
- **Lobbyist**: Advocate for interests to lawmakers; represent corporations, groups, or causes
- **Political Consultant**: Advise candidates and organizations; campaign strategy, messaging, polling
- **Intelligence Analyst**: Assess geopolitical threats and opportunities; government agencies, private firms
- **Government Official**: Work in public administration; policy implementation, management, public service
- **Journalist/Commentator**: Cover politics, analyze events; newspapers, broadcast, online media
- **Lawyer**: Practice law with political implications; constitutional law, civil rights, government relations
- **NGO Director**: Lead non-governmental organizations; advocacy, international development, human rights
