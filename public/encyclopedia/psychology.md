# Psychology

Psychology is the study of the mind, behavior, and mental processes to understand why people think, feel, and act as they do.

## History

- **Ancient Roots:** Philosophical questions about the mind in Greece, India, and China.
- **19th Century:** Wilhelm Wundt founds the first psychology lab (1879); psychology emerges as a science.
- **Early 20th Century:** Freud’s psychoanalysis, behaviorism (Watson, Skinner), and Gestalt psychology.
- **Mid–Late 20th Century:** Cognitive revolution, humanistic psychology, neuroscience advances.
- **Contemporary:** Integration of biological, cognitive, and social approaches; focus on mental health and well-being.

## Notable Figures

- **Sigmund Freud:** Psychoanalysis, unconscious mind.
- **B.F. Skinner:** Behaviorism, operant conditioning.
- **Jean Piaget:** Cognitive development.
- **Carl Rogers:** Humanistic psychology.
- **Aaron Beck:** Cognitive therapy.
- **Carol Dweck:** Mindset theory.

## Timeline

- **1879:** Wundt opens first psychology lab.
- **1900:** Freud publishes "The Interpretation of Dreams."
- **1950s:** Cognitive psychology emerges.
- **1960s:** Humanistic psychology (Rogers, Maslow).
- **1980s:** Rise of neuroscience and brain imaging.
- **2000s:** Positive psychology, focus on well-being.

## Case Study: The Stanford Prison Experiment

In 1971, Philip Zimbardo’s experiment showed how social roles and environments can dramatically influence behavior, raising important ethical questions and insights into authority and conformity.

## Knowledge Base

### Core Psychological Perspectives

- **Cognitive Psychology:** Information processing, memory, attention, problem-solving
- **Developmental Psychology:** Growth and change across the lifespan
- **Social Psychology:** Group behavior, conformity, attitudes
- **Clinical Psychology:** Mental health, disorders, therapy
- **Neuropsychology:** Brain-behavior relationships
- **Behavioral Psychology:** Conditioning, learning

### Psychological Concepts & Theories

- **Nature vs. Nurture:** Genetics vs. environment
- **Operant Conditioning:** Reinforcement and punishment
- **Cognitive Distortions:** Faulty thinking patterns
- **Attachment:** Early bonds, relationship patterns
- **Motivation:** Needs, goals, intrinsic/extrinsic
- **Emotional Intelligence:** Managing emotions

### Major Psychologists & Schools

- **Freud:** Psychoanalysis
- **Rogers:** Humanistic
- **Bandura:** Social learning
- **Skinner:** Behaviorism
- **Piaget:** Developmental stages
- **Beck:** Cognitive therapy
- **Dweck:** Mindset

### Common Mental Health Conditions

- **Depression:** Sadness, loss of interest
- **Anxiety Disorders:** Worry, panic, phobias
- **PTSD:** Trauma responses
- **Schizophrenia:** Hallucinations, delusions
- **Personality Disorders:** Maladaptive patterns
- **Substance Use Disorders:** Addiction

### Therapeutic Approaches

- **CBT:** Modify thoughts/behaviors
- **Psychoanalysis:** Unconscious conflicts
- **Humanistic Therapy:** Growth, self-acceptance
- **Systemic/Family Therapy:** Relationships
- **Medications:** Antidepressants, antipsychotics
- **Mindfulness & Acceptance:** ACT, meditation

### Research Methods in Psychology

- **Experimental:** Random assignment, control groups
- **Correlational:** Relationships between variables
- **Observational:** Natural settings
- **Survey/Questionnaire:** Self-report data
- **Case Study:** In-depth individual analysis

### Skills & Resources

- **Essential Skills:** Empathy, listening, analytical thinking
- **Learning Paths:** Courses, research, internships
- **Tools:** Assessments, observation, statistics
- **Ethical Considerations:** Confidentiality, consent, bias

### Fields of Work

- **Clinical Psychologist:** Diagnose/treat mental health
- **Counselor/Therapist:** Therapy, support
- **School Psychologist:** Student well-being
- **Industrial-Organizational Psychologist:** Workplace productivity
- **Research Psychologist:** Studies, publications
- **Forensic Psychologist:** Legal matters
- **Neuropsychologist:** Brain injuries/disorders
- **Health Psychologist:** Medical conditions
